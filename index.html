The funding ladder

  1. Fellowship — T32 / F32 individual fellowship
  2. Mentored K — K08 / K23 / K99 (3–5 yrs protected time)
  3. Transition — K99→R00, or K → R01
  4. Independence — R01 / R37 / DP2; ASH/ASCO/AACR career grants alongside

Society bridge awards (ASH RTAF/Scholar, ASCO/Conquer Cancer YIA & CDA, AACR career grants, LLS, ACS) commonly fund the gap between fellowship and a K.

Standard NIH K receipt dates

  • New Feb 12 · Jun 12 · Oct 12
  • Renewal/Resub Mar 12 · Jul 12 · Nov 12

AIDS-related and some FOAs differ — always confirm the dates in the specific funding opportunity.

K-prep checklist

  • Identify primary mentor + co-mentor/advisory committee early (12–18 months out).
  • Define a focused, fundable aims page; get it read by a K awardee in your division.
  • Secure institutional commitment letter (≥75% protected effort).
  • Line up a career-development / coursework plan (stats, responsible conduct, etc.).
  • Contact the program officer at your target IC (NCI/NHLBI) before submitting.
  • Budget for a strong preliminary-data and timeline section.
  • Plan letters of reference and biosketches with the "Contributions to Science" narrative.

    Where the grant data comes from

    The Grant Library and Overview are built from the NIH RePORTER API, refreshed weekly by an automated job. We query active career-development (K-series) awards: K08, K23, K99, K12, K38, K43, K01, K22, K24.

    • Oncology: all such awards administered by the NCI.
    • Benign hematology: NHLBI awards whose title/abstract/terms match hematology keywords (sickle cell, thrombosis, bone marrow failure, hematopoiesis, etc.), so we capture blood disorders without pulling in cardiology/pulmonary awards.

    How awards are classified

    Subarea is assigned by keyword matching on each award's title, abstract, and NIH terms (Leukemia/MDS/MPN, Lymphoma, Myeloma, Cellular therapy/BMT, Immuno-oncology, Benign hematology, Solid tumor, or Other). Research type is heuristic: K23/K12 and awards mentioning clinical/patient-oriented language are tagged "Patient-oriented / Clinical"; the rest "Laboratory / Translational." These are approximations — use the linked RePORTER record as the source of truth.

    Conference deadlines

    Conference data is manually curated and verified against each society's official call-for-abstracts. Deadlines change yearly; the "last verified" date is shown on the Conference Deadlines tab. Official sources:

    • AACR · ASCO · ASH · EHA · ESMO · SOHO · SITC · ASTRO · ASCO GI/GU symposia · SABCS · ASTCT/CIBMTR Tandem (each conference card links to its official abstract page).

    Limitations & corrections

    • RePORTER reflects funded NIH awards only — foundation/society/industry awards are not included.
    • Award amounts shown are the most recent fiscal year's funding for each project.
    • Subarea/type classification is automated and imperfect.
    • To suggest a fix, open an issue on the project's GitHub repo (see README).
